POST CAPTION (English):
Some towns survive history by fighting for it. Sarlat-la-Canéda survived by being forgotten. After the Wars of Religion drained its streets of trade and life, this Périgord town simply stopped — no demolitions, no modernisation, no grand renovations. For three centuries it stood still while the rest of France moved on. It took a 1962 law, the Loi Malraux, to officially "rediscover" it and protect what time had accidentally preserved. A few hours wandering these cobblestones is enough to understand what medieval France actually looked like — not a reconstruction, just the original, still standing. Have you ever walked into a place that felt genuinely out of time?
