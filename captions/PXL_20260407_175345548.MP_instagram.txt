POST CAPTION (English):
Estella-Lizarra wasn't discovered by the Camino de Santiago — it was purpose-built for it. In 1090, King Sancho Ramírez of Navarre founded this town from scratch to serve the flood of pilgrims and merchants flowing west along the pilgrim road. At its peak in the 12th and 13th centuries it was known as "the Toledo of the north" — wealthy, cosmopolitan, packed with Romanesque churches and a royal palace. We arrived by Land Rover, not on foot, and too late to get inside San Pedro de la Rúa, whose cloister is said to be one of the finest in Navarre. But even from the outside, those stairs and that tower make you stop and look up. The Camino has a way of doing that, even when you're just driving past. Have you ever followed the pilgrim road — on foot, by car, or any other way?
